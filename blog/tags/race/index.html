Mace's Hideout 100 DNF

June 3, 2023 · One min read
Jeremy Day
Ultrarunner

Race Report​

Well, this did not go according to plan. I started getting a migraine at the Greenhorn Aid Station at mile 18.3. That's the second highest point on the course. On the way down the mountain it mostly went away, but it came roaring back around Pole Creek at the bottom of the mountain. I pushed on to Sand Hollow at mile 37.7, but I was experiencing some dizziness. I decided I didn't want to risk vertigo further up the mountain, since Sand Hollow was pretty much the lowest point on the course, so I called it a day. Right after I dropped the skies opened up and a cold, cold rain started falling.

Royal Gorge Groove 50k Race Report

April 22, 2023 · One min read
Jeremy Day
Ultrarunner

Race Report​

I didn't actually get to run the Royal Gorge Groove 50k. Snow came in overnight and it was blowing way harder than expected when I left the house at 5:50 AM. I couldn't use my brights, because the snow just reflected the light back at me, like fog. Once I got south of Fort Carson heading towards Cañon City the roads turned to absolute shit. I'm talking drive-30-miles-per-hour-and-not-see-the-road-stripes shit. So I turned around, which involved some ice sliding when I slowed down further to turn onto a side street, and went home. I didn't feel even a little bit bad about missing the race, even though I was looking forward to a good run.

Rattler 50k Volunteer Report

April 8, 2023 · One min read
Jeremy Day
Ultrarunner

Volunteer Report​

I volunteered at this race in 2023, and it was a really well put together event. I volunteered at packet pickup on Friday, and Denise, the co-race director really had her shit together. We set up in the Colorado Running Company and had a pretty steady influx of runners the entire time we were there.

On Saturday morning I met Justin, the other co-race director, at the start line at 5:30 AM, and there were already a bunch of volunteers helping get things set up. I helped get the start/finish line and the aid station there set up and then we drove out to the one on-course aid station to get it up and running. Runners would hit that aid station twice per loop. The markings could have been a little better around the aid station to let runners know which way to go when they left, depending on the direction they came in on, but that's something to work on next year. I spent the rest of the morning there.
