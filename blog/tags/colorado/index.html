Mount Rosa Long Ascent

June 11, 2023 · One min read
Jeremy Day
Ultrarunner

Race Report​

I finally got around to summiting Mount Rosa. I started at 4:20 in the morning at the parking lot at the bottom of Cheyenne Mountain Canyon and finished 8 hours, 25 miles, and 6,725 feet of climbing later. I was expecting closer to 23 miles, and those extra couple of miles were rough, as I needed to stretch my water more than expected. I'm still getting used to long mountain adventures, and I told my wife I thought I might be done at 10:00. I felt bad about being completely wrong, but I had my phone with me and had turned on my Spot tracker, so she knew where I was okay.

Mace's Hideout 100 DNF

June 3, 2023 · One min read
Jeremy Day
Ultrarunner

Race Report​

Well, this did not go according to plan. I started getting a migraine at the Greenhorn Aid Station at mile 18.3. That's the second highest point on the course. On the way down the mountain it mostly went away, but it came roaring back around Pole Creek at the bottom of the mountain. I pushed on to Sand Hollow at mile 37.7, but I was experiencing some dizziness. I decided I didn't want to risk vertigo further up the mountain, since Sand Hollow was pretty much the lowest point on the course, so I called it a day. Right after I dropped the skies opened up and a cold, cold rain started falling.

Race Report​

I didn't actually run 50 miles. I was struggling pretty much out of the gate with energy to my legs. I'm not sure why, because I think I fueled properly. I didn't sleep much Friday night because I had that anxious tick where I was afraid I was going to miss my alarm, even though there were three other adults in the house where I stayed who would be getting up for the race.
